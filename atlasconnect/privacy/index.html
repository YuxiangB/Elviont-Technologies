Privacy

Privacy, explained clearly.

This policy describes how Atlas Connect handles information when it creates and operates a system-managed VPN on your iPhone.

Effective date: July 23, 2026

Overview

Atlas Connect routes device traffic through an encrypted Packet Tunnel managed by iOS. The app is designed to collect as little information as practical and does not include advertising or third-party analytics SDKs.

No advertising No cross-app tracking Credentials in iOS Keychain

Information Atlas Connect Uses

  • VPN configuration: the selected server, protocol settings, and the information required for iOS to operate the Packet Tunnel.
  • VPN credentials: connection credentials are stored in iOS Keychain and made available only to Atlas Connect and its Packet Tunnel extension.
  • Invitation access: an invitation code and a randomly generated installation identifier are sent over HTTPS for validation. The access service stores hashes rather than the original values.
  • Network information: public IP, approximate city and country, network interface type, server latency, and availability checks may be processed to show connection and location information.
  • Local app data: connection history, data-usage totals, app language, appearance, notification preferences, and related settings are stored on the device.
  • Operational diagnostics: redacted connection stages, status changes, error codes, and stop reasons may be saved locally to help identify failures.

VPN Traffic

When the VPN is connected, device traffic is sent through the selected VPN server so the service can route it to its destination. Network infrastructure necessarily processes connection information such as IP addresses, timing, and destination details needed to transmit traffic and protect the service.

Atlas Connect does not use tunnel traffic for advertising or cross-app tracking. Do not use the service for unlawful activity.

Storage And Retention

On Your iPhone

Preferences, usage statistics, and connection history remain in iOS-protected local storage until removed with the app or cleared by an available in-app control.

In iOS Keychain

VPN credentials and the invitation-access marker use Keychain protection and are not stored in ordinary preferences or plain-text app files.

Invitation Service

Hashed invitation and installation values may be retained as needed to validate access, prevent unauthorized reuse, and protect the service.

Temporary Files

Sensitive imported configuration material is moved into Keychain and the temporary plain-text copy is removed after a successful import.

Services And Sharing

Atlas Connect does not sell personal information. Information is shared only as needed to provide the VPN and app functionality, including with the selected VPN infrastructure, invitation validation service, public IP and approximate-location services, Apple system services, or when required by law or necessary to protect users and the service.

Your Choices

  • Disconnect the VPN at any time from Atlas Connect or iOS Settings.
  • Manage VPN configurations under iOS Settings.
  • Manage notification permission in iOS Settings.
  • Remove locally stored app data by deleting Atlas Connect from the device.
  • Contact support with a privacy or deletion question.

Security

Atlas Connect uses Apple NetworkExtension APIs, iOS Keychain, encrypted HTTPS requests, and encrypted VPN transports. No system can guarantee absolute security, but Atlas Connect is designed to avoid plain-text credential storage and to redact sensitive values from diagnostics.

Children

Atlas Connect is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children for advertising or profiling.

Changes To This Policy

This policy may be updated when the app, infrastructure, or legal requirements change. The effective date at the top of this page identifies the latest published version.

Contact

For privacy questions or support, contact Elviont Technologies through its GitHub profile. Do not include invitation codes, VPN credentials, private keys, or other secrets in a public message.
